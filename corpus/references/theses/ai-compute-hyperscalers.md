## AI compute & hyperscalers


> He treats the Mag7 mostly as **leading indicators** (capex guidance is the
> master signal) and prefers to own the upstream bottlenecks. Direct positions
> are selective.

### NVDA (Nvidia) — the GPU constraint, "center of everything"
- **Thesis:** Background infrastructure, not his primary trade. The NeoCloud
  thesis rests on NVDA's incentive to prop GPU lenders (CRWV/NBIS) to prevent
  Azure/AWS compute consolidation. Training monopoly (CUDA) intact 18-36 months;
  inference competition (TPU/MAIA) a 2026-27 story. Treats NVDA earnings as a
  *lagging* indicator vs. hyperscaler capex. His $6B optical investments
  ($2B each LITE/COHR/MRVL) read as a forward signal of architectural
  bottlenecks.
- **Stance:** Structurally bullish, "hold," rarely a direct featured long. "I
  prefer to own the supply-chain bottlenecks upstream."
- **May 30 supply-chain basket:** Recast Jensen Huang's "you're so rich now"
  toast as an "NVDA ETF" of upstream partners: TSMC/TSM, Micron/MU, Delta
  Electronics, and Amphenol/APH. Use this as another map of where NVDA capex
  wealth propagates, not as a new direct-NVDA entry signal.

### TSM (TSMC) — the foundry backbone, "safest compounder"

- **Tier:** High-conviction long compounder. Traded calls for 100%+ in Sep 2025;
  added shares ~$302.5 (Dec). Op income $62B (2025) → $86B (2026) → ~$104B (2027).
  "$1.5T; INTC $234B — 7x just for waiting."
